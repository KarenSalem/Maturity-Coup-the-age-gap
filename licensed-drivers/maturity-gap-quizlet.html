Parent quiz

Is your teen ready for real-world independence?

Teenagers can be smart, kind, capable, and still under-practiced at the everyday skills adulthood asks of them. This short quiz helps parents look at two things at once: what your teen can do on their own, and where you may still be carrying the load for them.

Answer honestly. There is no email required to see your result.

Before you start

The goal is not to grade your parenting. It is to notice the pattern.

Each question has two parts. First, rate how independently your teen can handle the situation. Then rate how often you step in, smooth it over, remind, arrange, or rescue.
  • Use 1 for rarely or not yet.
  • Use 5 for sometimes or somewhat.
  • Use 10 for consistently or almost always.
  • Your result appears after all 24 prompts are answered.

After your result

Read the score as a conversation starter.

A lower score does not mean your teen is doomed, and a higher score does not mean your work is finished. The point is to find the next right handoff.
Pick one area where your teen can carry more of the real-world load this month: planning a ride, making a call, solving a small problem, managing a deadline, or recovering from a mistake.
The goal is not to pull away all at once. It is to trade rescue for practice, a little at a time, while the stakes are still low.
This quiz is a reflection tool for parents, not a clinical assessment.
