# Quote pack

## Suggested headline options

- The age of delayed independence
- Why the driver’s license stopped being a teenage milestone
- The road got older

## Suggested deck options

- In FHWA data spanning 1963 to 2024, the youth share of licensed drivers shrinks while women move to parity.
- The clearest line in the table is not just more older drivers. It is fewer teen drivers and a delayed path to autonomy.

## Quote-ready lines

- Between 1963 and 2024, the share of licensed drivers ages 16-24 fell from 18.6 percent to 11.2 percent.
- Women rose from 39.6 percent of licensed drivers in 1963 to 50.5 percent in 2024.
- In 2024, the first female-majority age band is 40-44.
- Age 18 is still only 1.12 percent of all licensed drivers in 2024.

## Safe framing

- Strong claim: the data shows delayed licensing and a shrinking youth slice of the driver pool.
- Safer claim: the data supports a proxy story about delayed independence.
- Avoid overclaiming: the dataset alone does not prove all causes of generational autonomy decline.
